What's new in Nudge

1. The Hider now hides more stuff, better

I am expanding what the Hider hides, and now there's better coverage for Twitter, Facebook and Buzzfeed, as well as a bunch of new sites hidden such as Vox, CNN, dev.to, Amazon and Cracked, with plenty more on the way.

You can check out the full list here. (Click 'Show the sections that Nudge hides'.)

Sections being hidden are now replaced by a light rectangle, and have a button to show what's been hidden (e.g. Show Feed).

This is so it's more obvious what Nudge has changed on the site, and so that you know what exact sections have been hidden.

2. Nudge is the subject of academic research

I've been working with the University of Neuchâtel in Switzerland to look into the effectiveness of Nudge's interventions.

This has led to a short paper about Nudge by Aditya Kumar Purohit at the University of Neuchâtel being accepted to CHI '20, the world's largest conference about human-computer interaction (thank you Aditya!).

The paper writes:

"Our results show that Nudge (1) helped users to become reflective of their social media usage, (2) possibly decreased their time spent, and (3) made the experience more pleasant."

More to follow, hopefully. Get in touch at my email address below if interested.
